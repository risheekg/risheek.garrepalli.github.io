Reinforcement Learning & Generative Modeling

Diffusion distillation reframed as sequential decision-making.

  • Trajectory as Rollout: Framed the diffusion student as a policy and its denoising path as a rollout.
  • The Covariate Shift Problem: Identified that teacher–student trajectory mismatch is a standard imitation learning failure mode.
  • DAgger for Diffusion (DDIL): Proposed an on-policy correction that eliminates shift and preserves marginal distributions at intermediate steps.
  1. Garrepalli, Mahajan, Hayat, Porikli. “DDIL: Improved Diffusion Distillation With Imitation Learning.” arXiv 2024. [arXiv]
  2. Yasarla, Hegde, Han, Cheng, Shi, Sadeghigooghari, Mahajan, Bhattacharyya, Liu, Garrepalli, et al. “Generative Scenario Rollouts for End-to-End Autonomous Driving.” arXiv 2025. [arXiv]

Reliability & Open-Set Recognition

What does a learned representation bound about model competence?

  • The Oracle Analysis: Proved that open-set detection ceilings are set by what the representation preserves about the data manifold;not the classifier or non-conformity measure.
  • Raising the Ceiling: Minimized information loss by pairing discriminative representations with generative objectives.
  1. Liu*, Garrepalli*, Dietterich, Fern, Hendrycks. “Open Category Detection with PAC Guarantees.” ICML 2018.
  2. Garrepalli. “Oracle Analysis of Representations for Deep Open Set Detection.” arXiv 2022. [arXiv]

Representation Analysis & Training Objectives

The training objective is not just a loss function; it installs a prior over what the representation must capture.

  • Global vs. Local Priors: Showed that score matching forces global directional structure, while masked reconstruction forces local compositional dependencies.
  • Combining Priors (MAgD): Demonstrated that augmenting diffusion with masking recovers structural information neither objective captures alone.
  • AR vs. Diffusion LLMs: Hypothesized that diffusion's trajectory-level planning induces fundamentally different information organization and MoE specialization compared to autoregressive models.
  • Skip-to-Good-Part: Demonstrated preliminary evidence of this architectural divergence by analyzing layer-wise representational smoothness and computational redundancy.
  1. Garrepalli*, Kadambi*, Borse, Hayat, Porikli. “MADI: Masking-Augmented Diffusion with Inference-Time Scaling for Visual Editing.” arXiv 2025. [arXiv]
  2. Garrepalli*, Goel*, Agrawal, Lott, Lee, Porikli. “Skip to the Good Part: Representation Structure & Inference-Time Layer Skipping in Diffusion vs. Autoregressive LLMs.” arXiv 2026. [arXiv]

Generative Priors in Perception & Embodied AI

Generative priors as deliberate inductive biases for perception.

  • DIFT: Showed that coarse-to-fine refinement is the optimal Bayesian prior for optical flow, yielding computational efficiency naturally.
  • RoCA: Identified representational expressivity as the bottleneck for rare, cross-domain driving scenarios.
  • Generative Rollouts: Used generative rollouts to successfully reweight the training distribution rather than manually patching it.
  1. Garrepalli, Jeong, Ravindran, Lin, Porikli. “DIFT: Dynamic Iterative Field Transforms for Memory Efficient Optical Flow.” CVPRW 2023.
  2. Yasarla, Han, Cheng, Liu, Mahajan, Bhattacharyya, Shi, Garrepalli, Cai, Porikli. “RoCA: Robust Cross-Domain End-to-End Autonomous Driving.” arXiv 2025. [arXiv]
  3. Yasarla, Singh, Cai, Shi, Jeong, Zhu, Han, Garrepalli, Porikli. “FutureDepth: Learning to Predict the Future Improves Video Depth Estimation.” ECCV 2024.
