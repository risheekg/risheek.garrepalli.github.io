Reinforcement Learning & Generative Modeling

Diffusion distillation reframed as sequential decision-making: the student is a policy, its denoising trajectory is a rollout, and the teacher–student trajectory mismatch is the standard imitation learning failure mode. DAgger-style on-policy correction (DDIL) eliminates this covariate shift and preserves marginal distributions at intermediate denoising steps. The same on-policy rollout principle extends to generative scenario rollouts for end-to-end autonomous driving.

  1. Garrepalli, Mahajan, Hayat, Porikli. “DDIL: Improved Diffusion Distillation With Imitation Learning.” arXiv 2024. [arXiv]
  2. Yasarla, Hegde, Han, Cheng, Shi, Sadeghigooghari, Mahajan, Bhattacharyya, Liu, Garrepalli, et al. “Generative Scenario Rollouts for End-to-End Autonomous Driving.” CVPR 2026. [arXiv]

Representation Analysis & Training Objectives

Score matching and masked reconstruction encode complementary representational biases — what the model attends to and how it fills in missing information. Combining them (generative+discriminative at training time) yields richer representations and better inference-time recovery. This generalizes: across objective families, training formulation shapes internal geometry and determines what layers and tokens are load-bearing at inference.

  1. Garrepalli*, Kadambi*, Borse, Hayat, Porikli. “MADI: Masking-Augmented Diffusion with Inference-Time Scaling for Visual Editing.” arXiv 2025. [arXiv]
  2. Goel, Garrepalli, Agrawal, Lott, Lee, Porikli. “Skip to the Good Part: Representation Structure & Inference-Time Layer Skipping in Diffusion vs. Autoregressive LLMs.” arXiv 2026. [arXiv]

Reliability & Open-Set Recognition

What does a learned representation bound about model competence? Pairing discriminative representations with generative components — VAEs and normalizing flows explored independently — minimizes information loss and yields tighter PAC guarantees for novel category detection. The oracle analysis formalizes this: the ceiling on open-set detection is set by how much the representation preserves about the data manifold, and each generative+discriminative hybrid raises that ceiling through a different mechanism.

  1. Liu*, Garrepalli*, Dietterich, Fern, Hendrycks. “Open Category Detection with PAC Guarantees.” ICML 2018.
  2. Garrepalli. “Oracle Analysis of Representations for Deep Open Set Detection.” arXiv 2022. [arXiv]

Generative Priors in Perception & Embodied AI

Generative priors as inductive biases for perception and embodied AI: for memory-efficient flow estimation, for future-conditioned depth (predicting ahead as a generative prior over temporal structure), and for cross-domain autonomous driving where standard training cannot amortize the long tail — the bottleneck is representational expressivity and capacity for rare, distribution-shifted scenarios.

  1. Garrepalli, Jeong, Ravindran, Lin, Porikli. “DIFT: Dynamic Iterative Field Transforms for Memory Efficient Optical Flow.” CVPRW 2023.
  2. Yasarla, Singh, Cai, Shi, Jeong, Zhu, Han, Garrepalli, Porikli. “FutureDepth: Learning to Predict the Future Improves Video Depth Estimation.” ECCV 2025.
  3. Yasarla, Han, Cheng, Liu, Mahajan, Bhattacharyya, Shi, Garrepalli, Cai, Porikli. “RoCA: Robust Cross-Domain End-to-End Autonomous Driving.” arXiv 2025. [arXiv]
