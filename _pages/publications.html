Reinforcement Learning & Generative Modeling

Diffusion distillation reframed as sequential decision-making: the student is a policy, its denoising trajectory is a rollout, and the teacher–student trajectory mismatch is the standard imitation learning failure mode. DAgger-style on-policy correction (DDIL) eliminates this covariate shift and preserves marginal distributions at intermediate denoising steps.

  1. Garrepalli, Mahajan, Hayat, Porikli. “DDIL: Improved Diffusion Distillation With Imitation Learning.” arXiv 2024. [arXiv]
  2. Yasarla, Hegde, Han, Cheng, Shi, Sadeghigooghari, Mahajan, Bhattacharyya, Liu, Garrepalli, et al. “Generative Scenario Rollouts for End-to-End Autonomous Driving.” CVPR 2026. [arXiv]

Reliability & Open-Set Recognition

What does a learned representation inform about model competence? Pairing discriminative representations with generative components 'minimizes' information loss and improves open-set recognition. The oracle analysis captures this: the ceiling on open-set detection is set by how much the representation preserves about the data manifold, and minimizing information loss in representation raises that ceiling by combining complementary training objectives.

  1. Liu*, Garrepalli*, Dietterich, Fern, Hendrycks. “Open Category Detection with PAC Guarantees.” ICML 2018.
  2. Garrepalli. “Oracle Analysis of Representations for Deep Open Set Detection.” arXiv 2022. [arXiv]

Representation Analysis & Training Objectives

The training objective is not just a loss function — it installs a prior over what the representation must capture. Score matching requires the model to predict directional structure across the data manifold and results in strong global representations; masked reconstruction requires it to model local compositional dependencies. These are complementary structural priors on representation. The Skip-to-Good-Part result makes the structural consequence explicit: training objective determines information organization, smoothness across layers and tokens, and hence learning dynamics, potential MoE expert specialization, and compressibility of the model beyond inference-time speedup.

  1. Garrepalli*, Kadambi*, Borse, Hayat, Porikli. “MADI: Masking-Augmented Diffusion with Inference-Time Scaling for Visual Editing.” arXiv 2025. [arXiv]
  2. Goel, Garrepalli, Agrawal, Lott, Lee, Porikli. “Skip to the Good Part: Representation Structure & Inference-Time Layer Skipping in Diffusion vs. Autoregressive LLMs.” arXiv 2026. [arXiv]

Generative Priors in Perception & Embodied AI

Generative priors as deliberate inductive biases for perception. DIFT enforces hierarchical correspondence estimation: from a Bayesian view, coarse-to-fine refinement is the right prior for optical flow — the hierarchical structure is imposed, not suggested, and computational efficiency follows from the structure rather than motivating it. FutureDepth is among the first to avoid teacher-forced training in video depth estimation: rather than regressing to ground-truth depth at each frame, the model learns latent temporal dynamics through future-frame prediction and transfers that generative prior to the discriminative task. In RoCA, the bottleneck is representational expressivity: rare cross-domain scenarios cannot be amortized into the loss at standard training scale, and generative rollouts reweight the distribution rather than patching it with augmentation.

  1. Garrepalli, Jeong, Ravindran, Lin, Porikli. “DIFT: Dynamic Iterative Field Transforms for Memory Efficient Optical Flow.” CVPRW 2023.
  2. Yasarla, Singh, Cai, Shi, Jeong, Zhu, Han, Garrepalli, Porikli. “FutureDepth: Learning to Predict the Future Improves Video Depth Estimation.” ECCV 2025.
  3. Yasarla, Han, Cheng, Liu, Mahajan, Bhattacharyya, Shi, Garrepalli, Cai, Porikli. “RoCA: Robust Cross-Domain End-to-End Autonomous Driving.” arXiv 2025. [arXiv]
